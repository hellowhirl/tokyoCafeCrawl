Atmosphere

雰囲気

This café offers a comfortable neighborhood atmosphere and almost makes you feel like a local in Okinawa. There are instruments and Okinawa jewelry hanging on the walls as well as small potted plants and a giant paper mache of a tree. The afternoon is chilled out and you can enjoy a quaint lunch by yourself or with friends. The evening times are a bit livelier as customers are more likely to drink Okinawa sake (awamori) or be entertained by a live music performance.

お店に入るとまるで沖縄に来た感じがします。楽器が壁にかけてあり、張子で作られた木の飾りもオシャレで、店内外には観葉植物がたくさんあります。ランチタイムは落ち着いた雰囲気なので、一人でも入りやすく友達と一緒でも楽しめます。夕方以降はにぎやかになりお酒を飲みながらライブを聞ける日もあります。

Story

ストーリー

The owner is originally from Okinawa so she is very in tune with Okinawa culture, food and music. Her goal was to create a space for live events in Tokyo where people could enjoy Okinawa themes without having to travel all the way to the most southern part of Japan. The building used to be a factory for making books, so it’s a large space with a high ceiling. The owner showed me pictures of what the space looked like before which had crumbling walls and other broken down parts. She worked to transform the once industrial space into something beautiful and even used some wasted material to make the giant paper machete tree inside.

オーナーが沖縄出身なので料理、音楽、文化はこだわっています。カフェを始めた動機は沖縄カフェを東京まで届けたいということでした。東京にいながら沖縄料理と雰囲気を十分に味わえます。こちらのビル以前は工場でしたのでスペースは広いです。オーナーが当時の写真を見せてくれました。オーナーが工夫をしてオシャレにリノベーションし、今の店に変身しました。店の飾りはリサイクルを利用して作られている物が多くてユニークです。
